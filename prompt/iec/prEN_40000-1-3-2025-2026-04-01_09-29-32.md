## Introduction

Vulnerabilities are likely to emerge over time during the life cycle of a product with digital elements (hereinafter referred to as "product"), including its software and hardware components, despite adherence to cybersecurity principles defined in prEN 40000-1-2 [1].

It is important to prepare for those events by developing an effective strategy for the management of vulnerabilities during the product life cycle, including regular monitoring, testing, risk assessment, prioritization, remediation, and verification, as well as communication, which are all essential activities to discover and fix new vulnerabilities and maintain cybersecurity coverage.

This vulnerability handling document refines the activities that are part of international standards such as EN ISO/IEC 30111:2020 [2], EN ISO/IEC 29147:2020 [3] and ISO/IEC TR 5895:2022 [4]. It elevates, where appropriate, recommendations to mandatory requirements to ensure conformity with the Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) obligations. It integrates essential elements such as Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), regular testing and review activities, secure update mechanisms, and explicit requirements to support effective Coordinated Vulnerability Disclosure (CVD) and vulnerability-handling processes.

The rigor with which vulnerability handling activities are expected to be executed are expected to adhere to a risk-based approach, determined by the potential societal impact of a product's security failure. For example, the regular test and other monitoring activities are expected to be executed more frequently for products with higher criticality, and having a more detailed SBOM can improve the detection of vulnerabilities in components used by the product. The horizontal standards for the Cyber Resilience Act cannot be too prescriptive and thus require appropriate product risk-based criteria to be documented in the risk assessment and the cybersecurity plan for the product.

Identifying a product that can cause high-risk impact, which can be found across all categories covered by the Cyber Resilience Act, is integral to the regulatory risk assessment that needs to be conducted by the manufacturer. To support a risk-based approach, this document defines requirement enhancements.

The vulnerability handling activities defined in this document are organized into the following distinct phases:

- Preparation: Establishing policies, processes, and capabilities for vulnerability intake and disclosure.

- Receipt: Receiving and monitoring vulnerability reports from internal and external sources.

- Verification: Assessing the validity, applicability, and severity of reported vulnerabilities.

Remediation: Planning, developing, and testing fixes or mitigations.

Release: Distributing security updates and publishing advisories.

Post-Release: Monitoring effectiveness and applying lessons learned.

Manufacturers implementing this document will be equipped to demonstrate due diligence in vulnerability handling, maintain product security over time, and support transparency and trust across the digital ecosystem.

## 1 Scope

This document specifies the requirements and activities that focus on the process aspects of vulnerability handling applicable to manufacturers of products with digital elements (hereinafter referred to as "product").

The document is intended to support compliance with the Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) Regulation (EU) 2024/2847, particularly the essential requirements outlined in Annex I, Part II.

This document, with the appropriate requirement enhancements, is applicable across all product categories. Implementing these requirements enables a manufacturer to ensure security is maintained throughout the product life cycle.

## 5 Vulnerability handling

## 5.1 General

This clause defines the key activities and requirements for vulnerability disclosure and handling, providing manufacturers with a structured foundation for designing and implementing their own processes. These processes enable manufacturers to manage communication among stakeholders, proactively discover and collect vulnerability information and reports, and diagnose and remediate vulnerabilities before they become public, thereby preventing malicious actors from exploiting them.

## 5.3 [PRE] Preparation

## 5.3.1 General

The preparation phase lays the foundation for establishing appropriate vulnerability handling and disclosure processes and policies to support the subsequent phases, providing a structured, methodological approach to identify, verify, and address vulnerabilities, including communication. This phase also includes requirements such as identifying software and hardware components, focusing on creating the Software Bill of Materials (SBOM), and establishing a coordinated vulnerability disclosure and vulnerability handling policy.

## 5.3.2 [PRE-1] Policy on vulnerability handling

## 5.3.2.1 General

A manufacturer establishes and maintains an internal vulnerability handling policy to clearly define their approach for investigating and remediating security vulnerabilities. An additional important aspect covered is the testing strategy that should be defined to support effective and regular tests and reviews of its products. This policy forms a core part of the manufacturer's overall vulnerability handling process and is compatible with the external coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy as per 5.3.3. The intended audience for the vulnerability handling policy is the internal staff.

## 5.3.3 [PRE-2] Policy on coordinated vulnerability disclosure

## 5.3.3.1 General

The purpose of the coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy is to communicate the manufacturer's intentions, responsibilities, and expectations regarding vulnerability disclosure. A coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy encompasses both two-party coordination (e.g. between the reporter and the manufacturer) and multi-party disclosure involving multiple stakeholders. To support this, the manufacturer develops and publishes an external coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy and ensures the related internal activities are in place to effectively coordinate the disclosure of vulnerabilities.

In addition, to prevent attackers from gaining early access to sensitive information, reporters typically notify manufacturers privately, and refrain from public disclosure until a fix is available after an agreed-upon embargo period has passed. The receipt, verification, and remediation development stages are generally conducted within this embargo period, ensuring that vulnerabilities are addressed securely and responsibly by all the parties involved in the process.

## 5.3.3.2 Input

Vulnerability handling policy

## 5.3.4 [PRE-3] Operational security

## 5.3.4.1 General

Information related to vulnerabilities is generally sensitive information, as vulnerabilities can be used by malicious actors. All external communication, as well as the manufacturers' internal processes, need to take operational security into account. The confidentiality controls can be lifted after remediation and advisory information has been published. Depending on the organization and its products, confidentiality controls can be as simple as marking information with TLP (Traffic Light Protocol) or be supported by technical controls like access control.

## 5.3.4.2 Input

## 5.3.4.3 Requirement

NOTE 1 For additional guidance related to this requirement, refer to EN ISO/IEC 29147:2020 [3], 6.7.

NOTE 2 Operational security can be implemented in a way that maintains accessibility for submitting vulnerability reports, including the availability of an anonymous reporting mechanism.

## 5.4 [RCP] Receipt

## 5.4.1 General

The receipt phase outlines requirements for monitoring both internal and external sources, as well as for receiving vulnerability reports, including those derived from testing and monitoring, submitted by various stakeholders.

In this phase, the manufacturer actively monitors publicly available sources for vulnerability disclosures or information that may affect its products. Upon receiving relevant vulnerability reports or information, the data are compared against the SBOM and the list of hardware components integrated in the product. By cross-referencing these sources, the manufacturer can efficiently identify any potentially affected components or products.

This phase also covers the initial assessment activities needed to identify potentially affected components. Essential to this includes activities related to regular tests and internal reviews to proactively discover new vulnerabilities.

## 5.4.8 [RCP-7] Performing regular reviews

## 5.4.8.1 General

Maintaining a product's security is an ongoing task. This includes performing security tests throughout the product's support period. It also includes activities to reassess the threats and risks posed to the product due to changes in cybersecurity attack techniques or the way the product is used in practical conditions.

The needs regarding these regular tests and reviews depend on the type of product and its use. A simple product developed on demand for a specific customer and purpose, as an individual unit, might require very few actions, while a more complex product supplied in large quantities for exposure to the Internet might require stringent monitoring.

The security risk a product is exposed to is not static. It changes as attack methods or the threat landscape evolves. This can, for example, include knowledge of cryptographic algorithms' strengths.

It can also turn out that the product context is not correctly described. For example, the product's use or operational environment may differ from the description during practical use.

## 5.5 [VRF] Verification

## 5.5.1 General

The verification phase, often referred to as triage, includes requirements for determining whether the reported vulnerability affects the product. It also includes requirements for prioritizing the vulnerability based on vulnerability risk assessment activities.

The primary objective of this phase is to assess the validity, reproducibility, and applicability of the reported vulnerability. During this phase, the received information is reviewed to determine whether it is complete and whether the vulnerability is genuine and applicable to the product in question. If a vulnerability is assessed as relevant, the security risk analysis is performed to determine whether the risk associated with the vulnerability is to be mitigated and, if so, with what priority.

NOTE 1 During Multi-party coordinated vulnerability (MPCVD) consider the guidance provided by ISO/IEC TR 5895:2022 [4], 9.2.

NOTE 2 Whereas [VRF-1] Initial assessment and verification and [VRF-2] Vulnerability risk assessment are separated, all the activities listed can occur in parallel rather than in a sequential order.

## 5.5.2 [VRF-1] Initial assessment and verification

## 5.5.2.1 General

The clause describes the initial assessment and verification of the vulnerability report. The primary objective of this phase is to assess the validity, reproducibility, and applicability of reported vulnerabilities. The manufacturer evaluates the credibility and information provided in the report, attempts to reproduce the issue, assesses its impact on the affected product, and assesses the severity of the vulnerability. ## 5.7 [RLS] Release

## 5.7.1 General

The release phase includes requirements for deploying and sharing remediation, describing associated changes, and disseminating remediation information to relevant stakeholders.

## 5.7.2 [RLS-1] Security update release

## 5.7.2.1 General

This subclause addresses the activities of gathering all the artefacts to address vulnerabilities and disseminating security updates.

Any delay in applying security updates gives attackers time to exploit the product's vulnerabilities. Therefore, the timely distribution of security updates is essential. A secure update distribution mechanism ensures the integrity and authenticity of security updates. An automatic update distribution mechanism ensures that vulnerabilities are remediated without user interaction for the products that support automatic updates.

Such updates need to be provided in a secure manner, which might, for example, use digital signatures and/or secure download services.

Security updates are to be applied automatically or manually depending on the type of product, its technical implementation, its intended purpose, and its reasonably foreseeable use.

Providing security updates that do not change the product's other functionality reduces the risk of unintended or unwanted operational impacts in the user's environment. On the other hand, some remediations require significant changes that cannot be implemented without larger interventions into the product.

## 5.7.2.2 Input

- Tested security update or remediation as required per 5.6.4

## 5.7.2.3 Requirement

[RLS-1-RQ-01] Security updates shall be provided, unless otherwise agreed between a manufacturer and a business user in relation to a tailor-made product with digital elements, free of charge.

[RLS-1-RQ-02] Once a security update is provisioned, it shall be distributed in a timely manner.

NOTE 1 "Timely" in this context means that security updates that fix a critical vulnerability (i.e. risk for societal impact) are handled with appropriate priority by the manufacturer as established in 5.5.3.

NOTE 2 Expedite software updates to address critical security vulnerabilities.

NOTE 3 There are several distribution mechanisms, including automatic or manual. The product and infrastructure design need to provide an adequate distribution mechanism, taking into account the product's intended purpose and target users.

NOTE 4 For additional guidance for the distribution, refer to prEN 40000-1-2:2025 [16], 7.7.

[RLS-1-RQ-03] Installation instructions shall be provided for security updates unless distribution is fully automated.

[RLS-1-RQ-04] The mechanisms to distribute security updates shall ensure their authenticity and integrity.

## 5.7.2.4 Output

- Distribution of security update

Installation instructions resulting from [RLS-1-RQ-03]

## 5.7.2.5 Assessment criteria

Evidence of security update distribution

Evidence of implemented security update mechanisms for secure distribution of security updates.

Evidence of the implementation of automatic security update distribution mechanism for products capable of being automatically updated.

Evidence of installation instructions

5. 7.3 [RLS-2] Release information

## 5.7.3.1 General

This phase describes a process for publishing information about resolved vulnerabilities and the distribution of corresponding security updates. It includes detailed guidance on communication, publication, and coordination with users and other stakeholders. Publications such as advisories or release notes provide clear, comprehensive documentation to help understand the nature of the vulnerabilities and manage associated risks effectively.

## 5.7.3.2 Input

- Tested security update as required per 5.6.4

Policy on coordinating vulnerability disclosure (CVD)

## 5.7.3.3 Requirement

[RLS-2-RQ-01] Information of fixed vulnerabilities shall be published in accordance with the policy on coordinating vulnerability disclosure (CVD) as required by [PRE-2-RQ-01], and where appropriate, this information can be summarized or aggregated.

NOTE 1 For additional guidance on publishing vulnerability advisories, refer to EN ISO/IEC 29147:2020 [3], Clause 7.

[RLS-2-RQ-02] The publication shall contain at least, but not limited to, the following information to enable the target audience to decide if the vulnerabilities are relevant and how to remediate them:

description of the vulnerability;

vulnerability identifier;

information allowing the identification of the affected product;

- potential impact of the vulnerability;

- severity of the vulnerability;

- detailed information on how to remediate the vulnerability;

release date and update date.

NOTE 2 For additional guidance on advisory elements, refer to EN ISO/IEC 29147:2020 [3], 7.4.

[RLS-2-RQ-03] Information regarding fixed vulnerabilities shall be made available in a human-readable format.

[RLS-2-RQ-03-RE] Information regarding fixed vulnerabilities shall be made available in a widely recognized machine-readable format.

NOTE 3 Machine-readable format is specified in ISO/IEC 20153:2025 [17].

[RLS-2-PM-01] To give sufficient time to distribute the security update, full publication according to [RLS-2-RQ-02] may be delayed if all the following conditions apply:

An evaluation determines that the security risks of full publication outweigh the security benefits.

Information about a vulnerability fixed by the security update is made available to users in such a way that an attacker could not use this information to exploit the vulnerability.

Product users are informed about the date of public disclosure of all vulnerability information, as required per [RLS-3-RQ-02].

[RLS-2-RC-01] Vulnerability information from [RLS-2-RQ-01] should be made publicly available on the EU Vulnerability Database (EUVD].

NOTE 4 If vulnerability information is disclosed through the CVE program, EUVD automatically adds it to its database.

## 5.7.3.4 Output

Publicly disclosed information on vulnerabilities addressed by the security update

- Rationale for delaying publication

## 5.7.3.5 Assessment criteria

Evidence of published information on vulnerabilities addressed by the security update

Evidence of published machine-readable information on vulnerabilities addressed by the security update, if applicable

- Evidence of published information on vulnerabilities addressed by the security update at EUVD, if applicable

- Evidence of information on publication delay, where permission [RLS-2-PM-01] was utilized

## 5.8 [PRA] Post-release

## 5.8.1 General

The post-release phase focuses on activities that occur after the remediation and release of a vulnerability. It includes requirements for monitoring released security updates, capturing lessons learned, and reflecting on the post-release process to inform and improve future development practices.

## 5.8.2 [PRA-1] Post-release actions

## 5.8.2.1 General

This phase is crucial for ensuring the ongoing quality and effectiveness of the remediation. The manufacturer has the discretion to apply this action in a way that enables continuous improvement of their product-related processes.

## 5.8.2.2 Input

- Tracked and prioritized list of vulnerabilities with their assessed risks

Vulnerability remediation

## 5.8.2.3 Requirement

[PRA-1-RQ-01] Post-release actions shall be carried out, including:

Case maintenance

Monitoring of remediation

NOTE 1 Monitoring of remediation can be part of the overall 5.4.3.

NOTE 2 For additional guidance on post-release, refer to EN ISO/IEC 30111:2020 [2], 7.1.7.

## 5.8.2.4 Output

- Documentation of post-release actions and related plan

## 5.8.2.5 Assessment criteria

Evidence of post-release actions exists

Evidence on the execution of the post-release actions as specified in [PRA-RQ-01]
